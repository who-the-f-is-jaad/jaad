Pick of the Day

Midori Takada, Through the Looking Glass

1983

AmbientMinimalismJapanPercussion

Not ambient as wallpaper. More like ambient as a room you slowly notice you’re inside. Takada builds it from marimba, gongs and silence, then keeps shifting the walls when you’re not looking. It’s patient, strange, and quietly unreal.

Pick of the Day

Hailu Mergia & The Walias, Tche Belew

1977

Ethio-JazzFunkAddis AbabaOrgan

Addis Ababa with its collar open. Mergia’s organ slips between Ethio-jazz, late-night funk and hotel-band swing like it’s the most natural thing in the world. Easy to put on for one track, hard not to stay for the whole thing.

Pick of the Day

Laraaji, Ambient 3: Day of Radiance

1980

AmbientNew AgeZitherDevotional

Before new age became a streaming category, Laraaji made brightness feel physical. Zither, bells and long vibrations, produced by Eno, but glowing very much on Laraaji’s own terms. It sounds simple at first, then it starts doing something to the room.

Pick of the Day

Mulatu Astatke, Mulatu of Ethiopia

1972

Ethio-JazzAfro-LatinJazzAddis Ababa

Not jazz with Ethiopian colour added on top. Mulatu had his own thing entirely. Vibraphone, Latin rhythm and Addis swing move at this slow, confident pace, like the record knows exactly where it’s going and doesn’t need to hurry.

Pick of the Day

Konono No.1, Congotronics

2005

CongotronicsLikembéKinshasaDistortion

Handmade amplification pushed well into the red. Konono No.1 turn likembés, scrap-metal percussion and street-level electricity into something rough, hypnotic and alive. It sounds like it could fall apart at any second, which is half the thrill.

Pick of the Day

DJ Sprinkles, Midtown 120 Blues

2008

Deep HouseQueer HouseNew YorkSpoken Word

Deep house with the lights up a bit too bright. Sprinkles keeps the politics, the memory and the sadness in the room, instead of smoothing them out. Slow drums, soft keys, hard truths. A house record that actually has something on its mind.

Pick of the Day

Jan Jelinek, Loop-Finding-Jazz-Records

2001

MicrohouseGlitchJazz SamplesMinimal

Jazz reduced to dust, loop and surface noise. Jelinek makes a house record that barely seems to move, then somehow lets every crackle do the work. It’s quiet, obsessive, and much deeper than it first lets on.

Pick of the Day

Huerco S., For Those of You Who Have Never (And Also Those Who Have)

2016

AmbientDub TechnoLo-FiDrift

Club music after the club has faded out. Pads, low-end shadows, a groove you feel more than hear. Huerco S. makes the afterglow feel longer than the night itself. Put it on in the background and it probably won’t stay there.

Pick of the Day

Jlin, Black Origami

2017

FootworkPercussionIDMRhythm Experiments

Footwork with the floor pulled out from under it. Jlin folds percussion into shapes that feel impossible, all pressure, speed and empty space. It’s restless, physical music, but never obvious about where your body is supposed to go.

Pick of the Day

Equiknoxx, Bird Sound Power

2016

DancehallJamaicaElectronicMutant Rhythms

Dancehall stripped for parts and rebuilt at an odd angle. Equiknoxx leave space where the obvious drop should be, then fill it with bird calls, bass logic and rhythms that keep dodging your expectations. Still sounds weird in the best way.

Pick of the Day

Oren Ambarchi, Hubris

2016

Experimental RockKrautrockMinimalFree Jazz

One riff, stretched until it starts showing different faces. Ambarchi pulls guitar minimalism into krautrock pulse, free-jazz pressure and motorik lock, but it never feels like a theory exercise. It just keeps moving, and somehow keeps getting bigger.

Pick of the Day

Tirzah, Devotion

2018

Alternative R&BExperimental PopUKIntimate

Love songs with the polish rubbed off. Tirzah and Mica Levi leave space, ache and awkward little silences in the mix, which is exactly why it feels so close. R&B as a private conversation you weren’t sure you were meant to hear.
