Editorial copies, and album selections.

Pick of the Day

Midori Takada, Through the Looking Glass

1983

AmbientMinimalismJapanPercussion

Not ambient as wallpaper. Ambient as architecture. Takada turns marimba, gongs and silence into a room that keeps changing shape. Patient, ceremonial, almost weightless.

Pick of the Day

Hailu Mergia & The Walias, Tche Belew

1977

Ethio-JazzFunkAddis AbabaOrgan

Addis organ music with its collar open. Hailu Mergia and The Walias move between Ethio-jazz, hotel-band funk and late-night swing like the room never has to close.

Pick of the Day

Laraaji, Ambient 3: Day of Radiance

1980

AmbientNew AgeZitherDevotional

Before new age became a streaming mood, Laraaji made brightness feel physical. Zither, bells and long vibrations, produced by Eno but glowing entirely on their own frequency.

Pick of the Day

Mulatu Astatke, Mulatu of Ethiopia

1972

Ethio-JazzAfro-LatinJazzAddis Ababa

Not jazz with Ethiopian colour. Ethio-jazz as its own gravity. Mulatu folds vibraphone, Latin rhythm and Addis swing into music that walks slowly but never stands still.

Pick of the Day

Konono No.1, Congotronics

2005

CongotronicsLikembéKinshasaDistortion

Handmade amplification pushed into the red. Konono No.1 turn likembés, scrap-metal percussion and Kinshasa street voltage into trance music that sounds wired directly to the street.

Pick of the Day

DJ Sprinkles, Midtown 120 Blues

2008

Deep HouseQueer HouseNew YorkSpoken Word

Deep house with the lights up and the politics left in. DJ Sprinkles returns the dancefloor to memory, grief and queer survival. Slow drums, soft keys, hard truths.

Pick of the Day

Jan Jelinek, Loop-Finding-Jazz-Records

2001

MicrohouseGlitchJazz SamplesMinimal

Jazz reduced to dust, loop and surface noise. Jelinek makes a house record that barely moves, then lets every crackle and blur do the dancing.

Pick of the Day

Huerco S., For Those of You Who Have Never (And Also Those Who Have)

2016

AmbientDub TechnoLo-FiDrift

Club music after the club has disappeared. Huerco S. leaves only pads, low-end shadows and half-remembered motion. Soft, blurred, built for walking nowhere.

Pick of the Day

Jlin, Black Origami

2017

FootworkPercussionIDMRhythm Experiments

Footwork with no easy floor beneath it. Jlin folds percussion into impossible angles, all pressure, speed and negative space. Dance music as architecture under stress.

Pick of the Day

Equiknoxx, Bird Sound Power

2016

DancehallJamaicaElectronicMutant Rhythms

Dancehall stripped for parts and rebuilt sideways. Equiknoxx leave space where the obvious drop should be, filling it with bird calls, bass logic and wrong-footed swing.

Pick of the Day

Oren Ambarchi, Hubris

2016

Experimental RockKrautrockMinimalFree Jazz

One riff, many bodies. Ambarchi stretches guitar minimalism into krautrock pulse, free-jazz pressure and motorik hypnosis. It feels simple until the whole machine starts breathing.

Pick of the Day

Tirzah, Devotion

2018

Alternative R&BExperimental PopUKIntimate

Love songs with the polish rubbed off. Tirzah and Mica Levi leave space, ache and awkward silence in the mix. R&B as private conversation, half-lit and close.
